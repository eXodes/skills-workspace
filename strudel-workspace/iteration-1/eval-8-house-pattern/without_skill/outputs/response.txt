Sure! Strudel is a browser-based live coding environment for music, inspired by TidalCycles. Here's a house music pattern with drums, bassline, and melody:

```javascript
// Set tempo to 128 BPM (common house tempo)
setcpm(128/4)

// Drums - classic four-on-the-floor house pattern
$: s("bd*4, [~ sd]*2, hh*8")

// Bassline - deep synth bass
$: note("c2 c2 eb2 g2").s("bass").lpf(400)

// Melody - simple chord progression
$: note("c4 e4 g4 a4").s("piano").slow(2)
```

**Breakdown:**

- `setcpm(128/4)` sets the tempo. House music is typically 120–130 BPM.
- `$:` runs multiple patterns at the same time (like separate tracks).
- `bd*4` gives you a four-on-the-floor kick drum — the hallmark of house music.
- `[~ sd]*2` places a snare on beats 2 and 4.
- `hh*8` adds rapid hi-hats for that driving house rhythm.
- The bassline uses a low-pass filter (`.lpf(400)`) to keep it deep and warm.
- The melody cycles through a simple C major chord.

**Adding effects for a more polished house sound:**

```javascript
$: s("bd*4, [~ sd]*2, hh*8")

$: note("c2 c2 eb2 g2")
   .s("sawtooth")
   .lpf(800)
   .room(0.3)
   .gain(0.8)

$: note("c4 e4 g4 bb4")
   .s("piano")
   .delay(0.25)
   .room(0.4)
   .slow(2)
```

This should give you a solid foundation for a house track. You can experiment with different scales, note patterns, and effects to develop your own sound!
