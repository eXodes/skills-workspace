Add reverb with `.room()` and delay with `.delay()` — both chain directly onto your pattern.

## Reverb: `.room(N)`

`.room(N)` sets reverb level from 0 (dry) to 1 (fully wet):

```javascript
note("c e g b").s("piano").room(0.8)
```

Optionally control the room size (0–10) with `.rsize()` or using the colon shorthand:

```javascript
note("c e g b").s("piano").room(0.9).rsize(4)
// or equivalently:
note("c e g b").s("piano").room("0.9:4")
```

## Delay: `.delay(N)`

`.delay(N)` sets delay wet level (0–1). Control timing with `.delaytime()` and echo decay with `.delayfeedback()` (keep < 1 to avoid infinite feedback):

```javascript
note("c e g b").s("piano").delay(0.6).delaytime(0.25).delayfeedback(0.5)
```

Shorthand `"level:time:feedback"`:

```javascript
note("c e g b").s("piano").delay("0.65:0.25:0.5")
```

## Combined

```javascript
note("c e g b").s("piano").room(0.8).delay(0.5).delaytime(0.25).delayfeedback(0.4)
```

## Orbits

Reverb and delay are per-orbit effects. By default all patterns share orbit 1. Use `.orbit(N)` to give a pattern its own independent effect chain:

```javascript
// Piano with heavy reverb on orbit 1
note("c e g b").s("piano").room(0.8).orbit(1)
// Bass with delay on orbit 2
note("c2 g2").s("sawtooth").delay(0.5).delaytime(0.5).orbit(2)
```

This way changing reverb on the piano won't affect the bass.
