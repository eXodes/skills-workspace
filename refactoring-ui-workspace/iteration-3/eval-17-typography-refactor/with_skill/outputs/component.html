How to Design Better Interfaces

By John Smith  ·  April 15, 2026

Introduction

Good design is invisible. When a user interface works well, people don't notice it — they just accomplish their goals without friction. This article explores the core principles that separate mediocre interfaces from great ones.

Visual Hierarchy

The most important thing you can do to improve any design is to establish a clear visual hierarchy. Not everything on the page deserves equal attention. Some things are more important than others, and your design should reflect that.

White Space

White space is not wasted space. It gives elements room to breathe and makes content easier to scan. Resist the urge to fill every pixel.
